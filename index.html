What is 4 in a Row?

4 in a Row, originally published by Milton Bradley in 1974 as Connect Four, is a two-player connection strategy game played on a vertical 7-by-6 grid. One player takes the red discs and the other the yellow. Players alternate turns dropping a single disc into one of the seven columns, and the first to line up four of their discs in a row, column, or diagonal wins the game.

How to play

Each player chooses a color and decides who moves first. On your turn, pick any column that is not yet full and drop a disc into it; gravity pulls the disc down to the lowest empty cell of that column. Play then passes to your opponent. The game ends as soon as one player aligns four discs in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. If all 42 cells are filled with no four-in-a-row, the game is a draw.

Strategy basics

The center column is the single most valuable square on the board because more winning lines pass through it than through any other column, so opening in the center is widely considered the strongest first move. Look for double threats — moves that create two separate three-in-a-row lines, only one of which your opponent can block. Watch the parity of available rows, since odd-row threats favor the first player and even-row threats favor the second. With perfect play, 4 in a Row is solved: the first player can force a win by starting in the center, a result first proved independently by James Dow Allen and Victor Allis in 1988.

About this app

This app focuses on the unbeatable scenario: by default the Very Hard AI starts first, taking the theoretically winning side of the position. Very Hard is backed by a WebAssembly solver that uses bitboards, a transposition table, and an opening book, so it plays optimally from move one.
